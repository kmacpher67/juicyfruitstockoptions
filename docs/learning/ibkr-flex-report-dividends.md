### What the Codes Mean

In the `Code` column of your Flex Query:

* **PO (Post):** This represents the **Accrual**. It records that you are owed the dividend because you held the stock on the **Ex-Date**. It "posts" the expected amount to your account as a receivable, but it isn't spendable cash yet.
* **RE (Reverse):** This represents the **Reversal** of that accrual. On the **Pay Date**, IBKR reverses the "pending" entry (RE) and simultaneously moves the funds into your actual cash balance.

### Why are there two transactions per row?

It can look redundant, but IBKR uses this "double-entry" style to maintain a perfect audit trail for accounting and tax purposes.

When you see two lines for the same dividend (like the **ARAMARK (ARMK)** entries in your CSV), it is usually representing the transition from a "Pending" state to a "Paid" state.

1. **The Accrual Line:** Shows the amount you are scheduled to receive based on your position size (`Quantity`) on the `Ex-Date`.
2. **The Settlement Line:** On the `PayDate`, the system "clears" the accrual.
